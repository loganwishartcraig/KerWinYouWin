Sam Kerwin: Putting the Community Back Together

Sam Kerwin was born and raised in the Fair West Neighborhood on the western edge of the International District. He attended Zia Elementary and Jefferson Middle School before graduating from Albuquerque High. Growing up in Albuquerque has given Sam a unique homegrown perspective on the challenges facing our city. The City of Albuquerque is full of potential, but systemic problems plague our neighborhoods. A homeless population with no permanent solution, children growing up in poverty depraved of the education they need to reach their true potential, dilapidated shopping centers and empty lots allowed to stand without improvement for years on end. Sam Kerwin is running because he loves this city and he is tired of seeing these issues ignored. It is time our neighborhoods receive the attention they deserve. It's time we become invested in improving this area of town.

It's time we elect Sam Kerwin to usher in positive change.

Sam Kerwin's opponents are career politicos, they are running to push their own agendas. Sam Kerwin is running to push yours. A vote for Kerwin, is a vote for your ideas, your priorities, and your solutions. Sam is a true believer that the key to creating a stronger economy starts by building a stronger local community. Be part of that community and let's build our future together!

Invest in Early Childhood Education.

Albuquerque families are blessed with having a wide variety of childcare options. However, many families are priced out of sending their children to top quality early childhood education child care. This problem especially effectives poor, working class, and families of color. But it also effectives our entire city and state over the long term. If children are not giving the right conditions to develop cognitive skills, social abilities and healthy behaviors in the earliest years of life, it becomes increasingly challenging to develop the same skills later on. These are the very skills that help people get jobs later in life and become engaged members of the community.

Investing in early childhood education pays for itself when you compare it to the cost the city and state will have to endure due to the adverse effects of not educating children early on. Intervening in a child's education results in less remediation, fewer high school dropouts, less criminal activity, and a higher educated workforce. The National Institute for Early Childhood Education Research estimates that the return on investment is 7 dollars for every 1 dollar spent on early childhood education. The city of Albuquerque has proven that they can provide quality early childhood education, so why not continue to do so by opening 5 to 10 more preschools in strategic locations.

Find Permanent Shelters for the Homeless.

Homelessness is a major issue in Albuquerque. The truth is it costs the city less to house the homeless than it does to pay for emergency services or to pay for keeping our homeless locked up in our jails.

One existing program run by the City of Albuquerque, Heading Home, has found that it costs the city 31.6% less to house the homeless than it does to pay for emergency bills generated by them living on the street.

There are currently many programs serving lots of different niches within the homeless community. But all of these programs have different eligibility requirements in order to house a person. Sadly, there is no program where the only requirement to be housed is that you don't have a home. Lots of great work is being done to serve the least fortunate and most vulnerable members of our society. However, anyone living in the city knows we are a long way from eradicating homelessness.

Housing the homeless has proven to be cost effective, it's time we fund these programs to the point where we no longer see people sleeping in the alley. We all benefit when we take care of the least fortunate in our city.

Fill Vacant Dirt Lots into Small Parks and Community Gardens.

Walking around I see a lot of vacant dirt lots. It is a blight that has been bothering me for years. My vision is to fill in these dirt lots with small parks and community gardens: Little green spaces for everyone to enjoy. Small pockets where people can go to escape the stresses of a hectic urban life. Plant life helps decrease stress and increase the quality of life for everyone. Beautifying the city has a profound effect on people's psyches.

To be honest these dirt lots can produce a lot more than just goat heads and tumbleweeds. Why not turn them into places where we can grow food, and spend time with our family, friends and neighbors. By planting perennial fruit and vegetables we can create small edible forests right in the heart of the city.

There are so many vacant dirt lots that we can really get creative with their use. How about a zen garden? Or a sculpture garden? A butterfly garden? Or a simple xeriscape garden? Because these parks are so small and numerous their possible implementations are near limitless.
